Research Experience

    Department of Biomedical Engineering, Zhejiang University                 Ph.D. student 2016 - Pres.

    Nano-plasmonic biosensor coupling electrochemistry to monitor heavy metal ions with nano-arrays        09/2016 – present

  • Deposited anodic aluminum oxide (AAO) with gold nanoparticles through cyclic voltammetry (CV) to fabricate nano-arrays

  • Designed specific DNA strands that were sensitive to heavy mental ions and could link gold nano-arrays

  • Designed the reaction container with SolidWorks and had it printed by 3D printing technology in order to experiment efficiently and save samples

  • Assembled DNA with nano-arrays and used the biosensor to detect Hg2+ through localized surface plasmon resonance (LSPR) spectroscopy

  • Used electrochemical methods-differential pulse voltammetry (DPV) and square wave voltammetry (SWV) coupling with LSPR for Hg2+ detection to get higher sensitivity


    Optical biosensor of molybdenum disulfide (MoS2) with gold nanoparticles for explosive detection       03/2017 – 09/2017

  • Synthesized the composites of MoS2 with gold nanoparticles and evaluated the composites with transmission electron microscopy (TEM), scanning electron microscope (SEM), Raman spectra, etc.

  • Functionalized the nanocomposites with specially-designed peptides which could capture TNT molecules

  • Experimented with the biosensor to detect explosives by absorption spectra with an optical system

  • Analyzed the data with softwares (MATLAB, FDTD solutions, CrystalMaker, etc.) and wrote the paper
